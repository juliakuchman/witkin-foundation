Bernard E. & Alba Witkin Charitable Foundation

About

The mission of The Bernard E. & Alba Witkin Charitable Foundation is: "Do what can be done to improve society by helping children."

The intent of this mission is twofold. First, the Foundation provides financial contributions to charitable organizations in the East Bay region of the San Francisco Bay Area that support young children, ages birth through five years of age, so they are prepared for kindergarten.

Second, the Foundation provides financial contributions to charitable organizations located in the East Bay region that assist foster youth in becoming successful, independent adults.

To a lesser extent, the Foundation also financially supports East Bay elementary school programs which provide literacy, art, and music enrichment to elementary school aged children.

Foundation History

In 1982, Bernard E. (“Bernie”) and Alba Witkin established the Bernard E. & Alba Witkin Charitable Trust with funding from royalties of Bernie’s legal writings and publications, as to support non-profit groups advancing social justice and society.

Bernie and Alba's support spanned a variety of local and global initiatives in their early philanthropic years. The Witkins supported music in Berkeley’s public school system, affordable housing and food for the needy, gender and refugee studies, mental health services, and supported projects for peace, social justice, and human rights.

Following Bernie's death in 1995, Alba carried on the couple's charitable work. Alba’s concerns focused on the health, education, and enrichment needs of young children in the East Bay. Alba continued to support several law-related activities important to Bernie. In 2003, Alba formed the Bernard E. & Alba Witkin Charitable Foundation, annually contributing the maximum amount permitted under tax law to the Foundation. Alba was an active philanthropist until her death in 2015.

Currently, over 50 charitable organizations receive contributions from the Foundation each year. The Foundation looks forward to carrying on the work of the Witkins for years to come, and is open to forming partnerships with organizations that advance the mission of the Foundation.

Contact The Bernard E. & Alba Witkin Charitable Foundation

Mrs. Witkin enjoyed hands-on relationships with the organizations funded by the Foundation. Alba advocated for phone calls, face-to-face meetings, and site visits to become a part of our community's work.

In particular, Alba valued personal relationships with her fund reciepients and their beneficiaries. Many of these relationships lasted several decades, until Alba passed away in 2014. The Foundation keeps alive this spirit of building strong relationships with our community partners.
